This article is about the album by the German hard rock band Böhse Onkelz. For the neurotransmitter, see Dopamine. For other uses, see Dopamine (disambiguation).

Dopamin is the fifteenth studio album by German rock band Böhse Onkelz. It was released in 2002. The album was entirely recorded on the Spanish island Ibiza and mastered in the Abbey Road Studios in London.

Personnel Vocals: Kevin Russell Bass, Vocals, Keyboards: Stephan Weidner Guitar: Matthias Röhr Drums: Peter Schorowsky

Dopamine's chemical formula is C8H11NO2. It is an organic compound that consists of a catechol group ( a benzene ring with two hydroxyl groups) attached to an ethyl chain and an amine group.

Dopamine (DA, a contraction of 3,4-dihydroxyphenethylamine)

In popular culture and media, dopamine is often portrayed as the main chemical of pleasure, but the current opinion in pharmacology is that dopamine instead confers motivational salience;[6][7][8] in other words, dopamine signals the perceived motivational prominence (i.e., the desirability or aversiveness) of an outcome, which in turn propels the organism's behavior toward or away from achieving that [8][9]
